Methodology

Editorial standards.

How research on this site actually gets made, what sources it's built from, and what happens when something turns out to be wrong.

Primary sources first

Every company breakdown starts from the company's own filings — the 10-K, and where relevant the 10-Q or investor-relations materials — pulled from SEC EDGAR, not from secondhand summaries or other blogs. Each research page links to where you can verify the underlying filing yourself; that's deliberate, not an afterthought.

Figures are rounded, and approximation is disclosed

Where a number is rounded or hasn't been independently re-verified against the most recent filing at the time you're reading it, the page says so directly, usually in a disclaimer line near the top. If a figure looks precise to the dollar, it's meant to be checked against the source, not taken as gospel — markets move and filings get restated.

AI-assisted drafting, human-directed

Some drafting and research on this site is done with AI assistance, directed and edited by Marcus Mase rather than published unreviewed. Where illustrative or approximate figures are used for teaching purposes (for example, the fictional example company used in the financial statements guide), that's stated explicitly rather than presented as real company data.

Independence from advertising

This site runs contextual, programmatic advertising to help support the time it takes to write and maintain it. Ad placements do not influence which companies get covered or what conclusions a piece reaches — there is no sponsored placement or pay-for-coverage arrangement on this site.

Corrections policy

When a factual error is found — by me, or reported by a reader — the page is corrected and its "last updated" date changes to reflect that. Material corrections (numbers that change the actual conclusion of a piece, not just a typo) are noted directly in the piece. There's no policy of quietly editing a published thesis without leaving a trace.

Report a problem

If you think something on this site is wrong, incomplete, or misleading, I'd genuinely like to know — get in touch with the page and the specific claim.
