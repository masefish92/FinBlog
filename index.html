ValuationMoat

A structural reason competitors can't easily steal the profits — a brand, a network effect, a cost advantage, a switching cost. "Wide moat" in a vignette means: this pricing power is durable, model accordingly.

InsuranceFloat

Premiums an insurer has collected but not yet paid out in claims — cash it gets to invest in the meantime. Berkshire's entire structure leans on this. If a vignette mentions "underwriting profit," float is the subtext.

LiquidityRunway

How many months a company can keep paying its bills at the current burn rate before it runs out of cash. Shrinking runway plus rising burn is the setup for a dilutive raise — that's the cue.

M&AGoodwill

The premium an acquirer pays above the target's fair value of net assets — booked as an intangible asset. A vignette flagging a large goodwill balance is quietly asking whether it'll get impaired later.

EquityDilution

Your ownership percentage shrinking because new shares got issued — options, secondary offerings, convertible notes converting. Watch for "weighted average diluted shares" ticking up faster than net income.

OperationsWorking capital

Current assets minus current liabilities — the cash tied up running the business day to day. A vignette showing receivables growing faster than revenue is whispering "collections problem."
