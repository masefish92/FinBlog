Guide 01 — 12 minute read

Three statements. One company.

Companies split one financial story into three documents partly for accounting reasons, and partly because it discourages casual readers from looking past the first page. We're going to walk all three, line by line, using a single fictional company — Loom Coffee Co., a small regional roaster — so the numbers stay connected instead of floating in the abstract.

The seam

Three documents, one arithmetic thread.

If a company's ending cash doesn't tie to its balance sheet, or retained earnings doesn't move the way net income and dividends imply, that's not a rounding error — it's a filing worth reading twice.
