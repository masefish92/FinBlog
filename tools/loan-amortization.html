Tool 02 — Loan amortization

See what the loan actually costs.

Three sliders, one honest chart. The green layer is principal — what you actually borrowed, coming back down to zero. The red layer is interest — the cost of borrowing, stacked on top of it.

Illustrative model only — assumes a fixed rate for the full term and standard monthly amortization. Ignores taxes, insurance, fees, and extra payments.

Why the balance barely moves at first

Every payment is split between interest on what you still owe and principal that actually reduces the debt. Early on, the balance is at its highest, so the interest portion is at its highest too — a large share of your first few years of payments barely touches the principal. Drag the years slider out and watch the red layer balloon before the green layer ever catches up; that crossover point, where you're finally paying down more principal than interest each month, happens later than most people expect.

This is exactly what an amortization schedule is: a fixed payment, recalculated every month into a shrinking interest share and a growing principal share, so the loan hits exactly zero on the last scheduled payment. A shorter term or a lower rate shrinks the red layer fast — try halving the years and see how much total interest disappears, even though the monthly payment goes up.
