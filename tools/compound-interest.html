Watch compounding do the work.

Why the two colors matter

Most calculators show you one number and call it a day. The split matters because it's the whole argument for starting early: in the first few years, almost all of that green layer is thin — your own contributions are doing most of the work. Stretch the years slider out, though, and watch the green layer overtake the amber one. That crossover is compounding stopping being a rounding error and starting to be the entire point.

If you want the mechanism instead of just the picture: every period, the calculator adds your interest back into the principal, so next period's interest is earned on last period's interest, not just your original contribution. Compounding daily instead of annually barely changes the outcome at realistic rates — what actually moves the final number is time and the rate itself. Try dragging years and rate back and forth and compare how much each one bends the curve.
