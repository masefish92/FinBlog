Not investment advice

Nothing published on The Margin — including the glossary, the guides, and the company research pages — is investment, legal, tax, or financial advice. It's general educational commentary about how financial statements and public companies work, written for a general audience. It does not account for your personal financial situation, goals, or risk tolerance, because I don't know what they are.

No fiduciary relationship

Reading this site does not create a client, advisory, or fiduciary relationship between you and Marcus Mase or The Margin. I am not your financial advisor, accountant, or attorney, regardless of my professional background.

Do your own research

Company research pages state a thesis and argue both a bull case and a bear case — they are a starting point for your own thinking, not a conclusion to adopt. Figures are rounded and clearly marked as approximate where not independently re-verified against the latest filing. Always check the primary source — the company's actual 10-K on SEC EDGAR — before making any decision.

Position disclosures

Where I hold a personal position in a company discussed on this site, I disclose it directly within that piece. The absence of a disclosure should be read as no disclosed position at the time of publication. Positions can change after a piece is published without the piece being updated.

I try to get things right, and I correct mistakes when I find them or when they're reported to me — see the editorial standards page for how corrections are handled. I make no guarantee that any figure, date, or characterization on this site is free of error.
