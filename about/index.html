The Margin is a small finance publication with one idea behind it: most financial writing is built to make you feel dumb so you'll pay someone to translate it back. This does the opposite. Show the mechanism, name the trick, hand over the vocabulary, get out of the way.

Who writes this

The Margin is written under the name Marcus Mase.

I'm a CPA, and I work in tech — close enough to how large companies actually report their numbers to know where the framing gets chosen and where it's forced. I read 10-Ks for a living-adjacent reason, and I got tired of watching smart people bounce off financial statements because nobody would just explain the machine.

I write under a pen name because the work should stand on the reasoning, not on a résumé. But the credential is real, and it's here on purpose: if I tell you a goodwill balance is quietly asking to be impaired, you should know that's coming from someone who's actually sat with the standard, not someone who skimmed a summary.

I'm not a charterholder, not an advisor, and not selling you anything. (More on that below.)

What the site covers

Three stacks, depending on where you're stuck:

  • Glossary — plain-language definitions with the exact phrasing that should trigger them. No jargon defined with more jargon.
  • Guides — line-by-line walkthroughs of the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement. Annotated, not summarized.
  • Research — real companies pulled apart in public, using their actual filings, with the disagreements left in.

How the research gets done

Everything here starts from primary sources — the company's own 10-K, 10-Q, and investor filings, pulled from SEC EDGAR and company IR pages. When a number matters, it's linked back to where it came from. When something is my read rather than a fact, it's framed as a read.

If I get something wrong, I fix it and say so. Corrections are noted on the page, not quietly swapped — see the editorial standards page for the full policy.

What this isn't

It's educational. It's not investment advice, not a recommendation to buy or sell anything, and not a substitute for your own work or a professional who knows your situation. See the Disclaimer for the full version.
